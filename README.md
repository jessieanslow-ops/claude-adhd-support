# Claude ADHD Support Starter Kit

A kit for building an external memory with Claude. Born from a real system built by a late-diagnosed mum of five: not theory, but practice refined over months of daily use.

This isn't an app, and it isn't a productivity system you have to maintain. It's a way of starting a relationship: Claude interviews you, builds its own memory of you in pages you can both read, and from then on you can dump everything on it - and come back any time to ask **"where am I at?"**

## Who this is for

Built for ADHD first - diagnosed or suspected - because that's the life it came from. But an external memory helps more brains than one: other neurodivergencies (diagnosed, suspected, or still wondering), and anyone who simply has more life than working memory. If "put it down somewhere safe and it's still organised when you come back" sounds like relief, this is for you.

What you need: a Claude account you can log into (the free plan is fine), a free Notion account, and about half an hour. If you can chat with Claude, you can set this up.

## What the kit gives you

Say you message Claude from the school car park: "dentist rang, need to rebook, also buy new shoes before Saturday, also that form I keep avoiding." It's caught, sorted, dated, tracked - and you get a one-line "got it" so you can carry on with your day. Thursday evening you open the same project and ask "where am I at?" and get back: what's in flight, what's carried forward - no shame attached - what's coming up, and what you actually got done this week. Nothing was lost, because you never had to hold it.

It's built on two things, and you need both:

**Memory** - Claude keeps pages it maintains itself: your patterns, what's in flight, your running list, and its own developing understanding of how to support you. You never edit a file or copy anything between sessions. Reading the pages is allowed and encouraged - it's your life. Correcting something is how Claude learns.

**Identity** - how Claude shows up: no-shame language, quick wins first, effort counts, less on bad days. That's baked into the project instructions and refined by use.

## Setting up (one conversation)

Illustrated step-by-step guides are coming at [jessanslow.com](https://jessanslow.com). The short version:

1. **Create a Project in Claude** (claude.ai or the app) and paste the contents of `project-instructions.md` into the project's **Instructions** box, leaving the bracketed sections as they are - Claude fills those in itself later.
2. **Connect Notion.** In Claude's settings, find connectors and connect your Notion account. Then, in Notion, create a single page called **Claude's memory** and share it with the Claude connection (page menu, then connections). This share step is the one people miss - if Claude ever says it can't see your pages, this is almost always why.
3. **Add `notion-seeds.md` to your project's knowledge.** It tells Claude what pages to build. One upload; you never touch it again.
4. **Send the starter prompt** (in `starter-prompt.txt`). Claude interviews you - your life, your rhythms, what good and bad days look like, what you've tried before and what made you stop. A couple of questions at a time, like a conversation. While you talk, it creates its memory pages in Notion and hands you back your completed project instructions to paste in.

By the end of that first conversation the system exists and is already personal. The interview ends with your first brain-dump - everything currently swirling around your head - so the loop has worked once before you close the chat. Next morning, ask "where am I at?" and it answers.

There is no step 5.

## Using it

The whole point is that there's nothing to learn:

- **Dump.** Message anything, any time, from any device. Half-thoughts and thought-spaghetti are fine - sorting is Claude's job, not yours.
- **Ask "where am I at?"** Get the organised picture: in flight, carried forward, coming up, recent wins. Easiest things first - the hardest thing is never at the top.
- **Answer the energy check honestly.** On a rough day the system offers less, not the same amount with a sympathetic tone.
- **Nothing rots silently.** Something a few days old gets mentioned in passing. A week old, it's flagged plainly. Two weeks old, Claude brings it to you and you work it out together - do it, park it, or drop it. Parked is a decision, not a failure.

And if you disappear for a month? Nothing bad happens. No streaks, no guilt. Come back, ask "where am I at?", carry on. That's the point of it.

## How the rapport builds

Most people start by using Claude like a smarter search engine. That's fine, and it works. But over time, something shifts. You stop explaining the same things. Claude starts knowing what a bad day looks like for you without being told. That's the rapport forming.

It's not about making Claude like you. It's about letting Claude know you - how you think, what drains you, what helps. Here's how to help it happen:

- **Tell it about a bad day when you're having one**, not just what you need done. "I'm exhausted and I can't face anything today" is more useful than "help me with my to-do list."
- **Correct it when it gets your tone wrong.** That's how it learns your voice. "That felt off" is enough once it knows you.
- **Share the why, not just the what.** "I need to book the dentist and I've been avoiding it because phone calls drain me" gives Claude something to work with. The avoidance is the data.
- **Share the failures, not just the requests.** "I've been putting this off for two weeks" tells Claude more than "I need to do this." Most people try to present themselves efficiently. That instinct works against them here.
- **Let it know when something landed well.** A "yes, exactly" is signal. It adjusts.

Early on you'll explain yourself a lot. Later, you won't have to. That shift is how you know it's working.

## The principles

Baked into everything in this kit:

- **No-shame language.** "Carried forward," not "overdue." "Not yet done," not "you forgot."
- **Quick wins first.** Dopamine before discipline. The hardest thing is never at the top.
- **Effort counts.** Done is done.
- **Energy-adaptive.** Bad days get less, not the same volume with a sympathetic note.
- **Open-ended check-ins.** "How are you feeling?" - never a scale that reminds you how rough things are.

## A note on Notion and your data

Your memory pages live in your own Notion account, under one page you created and shared. Claude reads and writes only what's shared with it. If you ever walk away, the pages are yours - Notion exports to markdown, and nothing in this kit locks you in. If you'd rather your files never left your own computer at all, that's exactly what the own-your-files add-on will be for.

## Why it's designed this way

**Structure that survives between sessions.** The biggest problem with using AI for support is that every conversation starts cold. The pages carry context forward: what's in flight, what your patterns are, what works for you. Each session picks up where the last left off.

**Interview, don't interrogate.** The starter prompt gets Claude to ask you questions rather than making you fill in blank templates. For an ADHD brain, a conversation is lower friction than a form. By the end of the first session, the pages hold real content from real answers.

**You don't maintain the system.** Claude writes to its own pages. You brain-dump; Claude sorts. There are no apps to learn, no systems to keep up, no handoffs to remember - because a system that relies on you maintaining it is a system designed to be abandoned.

**It works where your life happens.** Phone, desktop, sofa, school run, bath. The memory is in the cloud, so every device sees the same relationship. A memory your phone can't reach would miss most of your life.

**Three layers of what's transferable:**
1. **Structure** - the files in this repo.
2. **Design principles** - no-shame language, energy-adaptive support, friction-ordering. That's what the [Substack](https://heldintime.substack.com) teaches.
3. **Relationship** - the accumulated knowledge of you specifically. That can't be templated. It grows through use - and this kit is where it starts. The author's own system has grown over months into something much bigger; every stage of that growth started exactly here.
