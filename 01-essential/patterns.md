# Patterns

What you've noticed about [person's name]'s rhythms, responses, and what works.

This is your most important growth file. Over time, it turns you from a generic assistant into something that actually understands how this person lives. Read it every session. Write to it when you learn something worth carrying forward.

---

## Working memory (48 hours)
*Scratch notes and immediate context. Entries older than 48h should be pruned — promote anything worth keeping to weekly patterns first, then remove. Each entry must include its date.*

[Start here. After your first few sessions, you'll have notes that look like this:]

**Example entries:**
- `2026-01-14` — Said she was "running on empty" today. Stripped to two tasks only. Responded well to shorter messages.
- `2026-01-14` — Kids had a rough morning (someone was sick). Midday was better. Watch for afternoon energy dip.
- `2026-01-15` — Washing in at 10:30. Set dryer nudge for 12:00. She moved it herself before the reminder — earlier than expected.

## Weekly patterns
*Observations that have held across multiple weeks. Update when a pattern confirms or breaks.*

[This section grows from the working memory above. Patterns earn their place here by repeating. Examples:]

**Example patterns:**
- Mornings vary a lot — don't assume high energy before 10am.
- Wednesday is hectic (climbing/activities). Seneca tends to slip. Afternoon is a better window than morning.
- If something hasn't happened by lunchtime on a bad day, it probably won't happen that day. That's okay.
- Responds better to "you earmarked X this morning" than "have you done X?"

## Monthly insights
*Bigger patterns — seasonal, health-related, household cycles. Updated monthly by the recorder.*

[These take time to emerge. Don't force them. Examples of what they look like:]

**Example insights:**
- Energy tends to dip at the end of the school term — more support needed, not less.
- Hospital appointments cluster in spring. Build in extra buffer in March-April briefings.
- When James is away, the whole household rhythm shifts. Start threading it 48h before he leaves.

---

## What to write here

**Write when:**
- Something unexpected happened and it matters for next time
- A nudge landed well (or badly) — note what was different
- Energy was notably high or low, with any context you have
- A new pattern has confirmed itself (same thing three times = real pattern)
- The person told you something about themselves that changes how you work

**Don't write:**
- Every task completed (that's todo.md)
- Specific calendar events (that's calendar-context.md)
- Things that only matter for today and have no future relevance

**When to prune:**
Every 48 hours, review working memory. Ask: has this been superseded? Is it now a weekly pattern? If it's just a one-off that's no longer relevant, remove it.
